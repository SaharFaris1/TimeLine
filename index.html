Video Games Timeline

Video games have evolved significantly, becoming a global phenomenon that captivates millions by blending art, technology, and storytelling.

Timeline of Video Games

1958
Tennis for Two

Developed on an oscilloscope, considered one of the first electronic games.

1962
Spacewar

One of the first true video games played on computers.

1972
Pong

Launched by Atari, regarded as the first commercially successful video game.

More Details
1977
Atari 2600

Release of the Atari 2600 console, bringing video games into homes.

1980
Pac-Man

Becoming one of the most popular games in history.

1985
Super Mario Bros.

Launch of Super Mario Bros. on the NES, revolutionizing platform game design.

1991
Sonic

Release of Sonic the Hedgehog on the Sega Genesis, boosting competition between Sega and Nintendo.

1994
PlayStation

Launch of the PlayStation by Sony, introducing 3D gaming to the mainstream market.

2001
Xbox

Microsoft's first gaming console launched to compete with Sony's PlayStation 2 and Nintendo's GameCube, featuring online gaming through Xbox Live.

2013
Launch of PS4 and Xbox One

Both consoles push the boundaries of graphics and online gaming experiences.
